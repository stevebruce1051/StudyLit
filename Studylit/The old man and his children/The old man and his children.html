THE OLD MAN AND HIS CHILDREN

SUMMARY

Below are some examples of literary devices found in this short story

  • Personification:
    "You could not break the sticks when they were tied together,for then, they were strong.

    But how easily you broke them when they were separated,for then,they were weak."

    The sticks are described as having human traits-strength and weakness.
  • Simile:
    "It is the same with people as with sticks"

    "On a its fig-like fruit, mouse-birds and green parrots were quietly feeding."
  • Hyperbole:
    "The sight of Njoroge coolly continuing with his game of hoop-stick was the last straw"
  • Point of view:Third person narrative.
  • Symbolism:
    In the story, Kamau represents a man of wisdom.

    Also,the sticks that are together symbolises strength whereas the sticks that were separated represents weakness.
  • Oxymoron:
    "At this, the twins' mother rushed out of her hut to pull the boys apart, box their ears and give them a good scolding."
  • Onomatopoeia:
    "Njoroge flung himself down beside the old man's stool."

    "It went spining madly away and Njoroge shouted and lashed out angrily at his brother."
